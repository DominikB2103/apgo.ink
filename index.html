manifesto

Not a fund page. Not a startup page. A compounding machine.

apgo.ink is the public surface of Tannenhof Mayer: a black-and-white, high-conviction organisation for learning, allocating, building, and owning. We treat capital as a product, operations as leverage, and time as the hardest asset.

thesis

Where finance turns into formation.

Markets are not the endpoint. They are the sensor layer. Tannenhof Mayer uses financial intelligence to decide what should be owned, built, or ignored.

01

Permanent capital mindset

We look for decisions that improve with time: durable demand, operational learning curves, defensible distribution, and disciplined downside control.

02

Financial research

Models, memos, signals, and risk maps before allocation.

03

Company building

When an opportunity is better built than bought, we design the operating company ourselves.

04

Operating leverage

Tools, workflows, dashboards, automations, and taste compound faster than decks.

operating model

A four-lane engine for going from zero to owned outcomes.

Everything is static, public, and legible on the outside; internally the work is iterative, analytical, and intensely operational.

Observe

Find unfair facts.

Follow markets, filings, people, software, incentives, and cultural shifts until a pattern becomes underwriting-grade.

Underwrite

Price the asymmetry.

Define the downside first, then the upside routes, catalysts, liquidity needs, and reasons to walk away.

Build

Turn insight into asset.

When the best trade is creation, assemble product, distribution, governance, and operations around the thesis.

Compound

Let excellence repeat.

Recycle learning into systems, dashboards, standards, and capital allocation decisions that become more precise over time.

focus areas

Small team. Broad aperture. Sharp filters.

Tannenhof Mayer is intentionally early. The site is designed to feel like the beginning of a serious institution: spare, memorable, and ready to expand without redesigning everything later.

current lens

Markets as sensors

We read markets as high-frequency truth machines, but never worship price. The goal is to convert signal into ownership, not noise into activity.

principles

The house rules.

Designed as a long-term institution, not a moodboard. The visual system stays minimal so the thinking can become dense.

01

Risk is designed, not discovered.

Every thesis starts with what can break, who pays, how liquidity disappears, and what information would change the decision.

02

Ownership beats attention.

The organisation should prefer compounding assets, repeatable systems, and boring advantages over the dopamine of activity.

03

Craft is alpha.

Writing, design, code, analysis, negotiations, hiring, and governance are all part of the same return engine.

04

Start from zero, behave like permanence.

The earliest days should already contain the standards of the institution we want to become.
