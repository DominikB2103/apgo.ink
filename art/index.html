manifesto

No gate. No approved genre. No single taste.

We keep the page monochrome, editorial, physical, and sharp so the art does not get trapped inside a trend. The design is the room. The work can be anything.

all art

Total expressivity, not a department.

Tannenhof Mayer Art is for the full spectrum: refined, chaotic, technical, handmade, performative, commercial, anti-commercial, sincere, absurd, silent, loud.

01

Image is welcome.

Painting, drawing, photography, scans, posters, collage, diagrams, typography, raw screenshots, printed mistakes, and one-page visual worlds.

02

Performance is welcome.

The body can be the medium. Gesture, theater, dance, endurance, speech, silence, rituals, characters, social actions.

03

Systems are welcome.

Code, generative work, game loops, interfaces, strange tools, archives, maps, rules, machines, spreadsheets that start behaving like poems.

04

Object is welcome.

Furniture, fashion, sculpture, ceramics, found things, rooms, packaging, weapons of tenderness, props for impossible lives.

05

Text is welcome.

Poetry, criticism, captions, scripts, manifestos, lists, margins, receipts, inventories, letters, titles that do half the work.

principles

The room protects expression. It does not tame it.

01

No forced elegance.

Beautiful can be broken, ridiculous, rough, obsessive, technical, soft, or too loud.

02

No medium hierarchy.

A chair, a sound, a game, a photograph, a costume, and a sentence can sit at the same table.

03

No premature explanation.

Let the work arrive before the label. Let the category be late.

04

Small color, real signal.

Color is used like a pin, alarm, bruise, temperature, or door handle — never wallpaper.

open call

Work, sketches, photos, object studies, performance ideas, sound notes, code experiments, text fragments, impossible proposals, ugly first versions, finished pieces, or something in between.
