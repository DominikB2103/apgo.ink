Energy monotonicity principle

A theorem brief on residual-controlled energy hierarchies and the conditions under which monotonicity survives perturbation.

The principle records a familiar but powerful estimate: controlled residuals preserve the practical monotonicity of an energy functional across an interval.

Statement

Let E be an absolutely continuous energy functional and let R be a non-negative residual term. If the differential loss of E is bounded by R, then for every pair of times t₁ ≤ t₂ the following estimate holds.

E(t₂) ≤ E(t₁) + ∫t₁t₂ R(s) ds

Proof sketch

Integrate the differential inequality over the interval and use absolute continuity to identify the endpoint difference. The statement becomes useful when the residual admits a scale-invariant bound.

Example use

In perturbative stability problems, the residual term can often be separated into a small structural term and a measured error term, making the estimate suitable for iteration.
