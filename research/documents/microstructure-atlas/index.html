Microstructure atlas: measured phase boundaries

A discovery note describing a compact visual record of phase-boundary behavior across a controlled sample series.

Published
2026-05-04
Editor
Field desk
Section
Discoveries

The atlas records a sequence of boundary transitions observed under fixed preparation conditions. The point is not spectacle, but comparability.

Observation

Across the sample series, boundaries remain sharply defined until the final preparation step, where a diffuse band appears along the upper interface. The band is weak but repeatable.

Interpretation

The conservative interpretation is that the preparation step changes the local stress profile. Further measurements should vary one condition at a time and preserve the same imaging geometry.

Next reading

A later version of this note can include a measurement table, full-resolution plates, and uncertainty estimates for each boundary trace.
