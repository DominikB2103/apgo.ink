Theorem · Brief

Noether symmetry, stated for working readers

A short theorem page demonstrating how to present a mathematical statement with assumptions, intuition, and a proof sketch while preserving editorial restraint.

Published: 18 April 2026Tags: symmetry · variational methods · physicsStatus: example record
On this pageStatementIntuitionProof sketchUse

Statement

For a differentiable action functional, every continuous symmetry of the action determines a conserved quantity along solutions of the Euler–Lagrange equations.

Intuition

The theorem turns invariance into bookkeeping. When the action does not change under a smooth transformation, the corresponding directional derivative vanishes, and a conserved expression emerges along physical trajectories.

symmetry of action → conservation law

Proof sketch

Differentiate the invariant action along the one-parameter symmetry. After integration by parts, the Euler–Lagrange term vanishes on solutions, leaving a boundary term that must remain constant. The exact form depends on the coordinates and Lagrangian.

Use in the archive

Theorem briefs should be shorter than papers and more rigorous than blog posts. They are useful when the reader needs a clear statement, minimal notation, and a path toward a complete reference.

Editorial rule: a theorem page should separate the statement from the intuition. Mixing them weakens both.
