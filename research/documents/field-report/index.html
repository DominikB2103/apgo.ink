Discovery / systems

Field report on nonlinear stability and narrow perturbations.

A short dispatch showing how a local stability observation should be recorded before a stronger global interpretation is attempted.

Observation

A narrow family of perturbations returned to equilibrium faster than the baseline numerical model predicted. The effect was consistent across the recorded runs, but the sampling range remained deliberately small.

Interpretation

The observation suggests local damping may be stronger than assumed in the simplified model. It does not yet justify a global claim, because no wide perturbation survey has been completed.

Editorial caution

The language of this note is intentionally limited. A precise weak claim is more useful than an impressive broad claim that the evidence cannot support.

Open test

The next test should vary initial conditions, perturbation amplitude, and integration tolerance independently. A result that survives these changes may deserve a stronger theorem-facing formulation.

Record format

Future entries should record model parameters, computational environment, stopping criteria, and known failure cases. This makes the report useful even if the initial interpretation later changes.
