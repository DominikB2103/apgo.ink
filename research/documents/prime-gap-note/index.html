Prime-gap note: density, scale, and obstruction

An editorial note framing prime-gap estimates through scale, density heuristics, and the limits of elementary obstruction arguments.

Published
2026-04-21
Editor
Number theory desk
Section
Documents

Prime gaps are easiest to misread when the local picture is treated as though it were a global law. This note separates density, scale, and obstruction.

Density

At large height, average spacing gives a first-order expectation, but it does not predict the local geometry of any particular interval. A useful note keeps these two claims apart.

Scale

The scale of observation determines which irregularities are visible. Too narrow a window turns noise into narrative; too broad a window can erase the obstruction one meant to study.

Obstruction

Elementary divisibility patterns explain many exclusions, but they do not by themselves describe the full distribution. The editorial value lies in making the limitation explicit.
