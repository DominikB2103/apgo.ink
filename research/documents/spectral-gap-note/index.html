A short note on spectral gaps in sparse operators

A compact demonstration article showing how a discovery record can be written with clear assumptions, limited claims, and enough mathematical structure to feel credible.

Published: 4 May 2026Tags: operator theory · spectra · analysisStatus: illustrative draft
On this pageAbstractSetupObservationLimitations

Abstract

We consider a sparse self-adjoint perturbation of a bounded reference operator and record a simple stability condition under which an isolated interval in the spectrum remains open. The note is written as a template for concise discovery records rather than as a complete paper.

Setup

Let H be a separable Hilbert space, let A be a bounded self-adjoint operator, and let B be a sparse perturbation with controlled norm. The editorial goal is to state the model with enough precision that later readers can reproduce the claim.

If dist(λ, σ(A)) > ||B||, then λ ∉ σ(A + B).

The statement is deliberately modest. It avoids suggesting novelty where the purpose is documentation, verification, and internal reuse.

Observation

The gap persists whenever the perturbation radius is smaller than the distance from the candidate point to the unperturbed spectrum. In practice, this yields a useful sanity check for numerical experiments on sparse models.

Limitations

This placeholder record does not establish an optimal perturbation threshold, nor does it address unbounded operators. A full version should include references, exact hypotheses, and reproducible computations.

House style: end discovery notes by saying what the result does not claim. It is a strong credibility signal.
