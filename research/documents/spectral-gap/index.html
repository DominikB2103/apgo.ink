Spectral gap criterion for stable operators

A concise theorem page on eigenvalue separation, compact operators, and the role of curvature-like lower bounds.

The criterion gives a compact way to record when the first non-trivial spectral separation can be forced by a structural lower bound.

Statement

Let an operator satisfy the compactness assumptions required for a discrete low spectrum. Under a lower structural bound κ and a compatible normalization, the first spectral gap is bounded below by a constant multiple of κ².

λ₂ − λ₁ ≥ c · κ²

Use

The value of the criterion is diagnostic. It points to which assumptions are doing real work: compactness provides discreteness, while the lower bound provides separation.

Caution

The constant is not ornamental. In applications, a weak or poorly tracked constant can make the estimate formally correct but practically useless.
