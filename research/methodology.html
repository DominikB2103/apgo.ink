Method

Transparent by default, restrained by design.

Readers should understand how confident a claim is, what evidence supports it, and where judgement begins. Meridian separates source quality, triangulation, recency, and model sensitivity.

01

Evidence grading

Findings are classified by source quality, triangulation strength, recency, and sensitivity to model assumptions.

02

Review trace

Authoring, specialist review, editorial sign-off, and correction tracking are treated as separate stages.

03

Decision relevance

Each finding is linked to the practical institutional choices it may affect, not just the literature it summarizes.

04

Uncertainty language

Reports distinguish observed evidence, inference, plausible scenarios, expert judgement, and unresolved disagreement.

Evidence grade scale

A compact system for reading confidence quickly.

The grade never replaces the argument. It is a reading aid that forces the publication to show its evidentiary posture.

GradeMeaning
HighMultiple strong sources, direct observations, stable assumptions, and low disagreement.
Moderate–highStrong evidence base with some uncertainty around timing, scale, or transferability.
ModerateReasonable triangulation but meaningful dependence on model assumptions or incomplete data.
DevelopingUseful early signal that requires further review before being treated as settled.

Correction protocol

Corrections are part of the product, not a reputational footnote.

Every dossier can include release notes, errata, methodological changes, and source classification updates. Static pages make these records durable and easy to audit.
