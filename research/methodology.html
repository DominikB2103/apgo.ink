Methodology

Evidence grading, review protocol, and uncertainty language.

A serious research website needs a visible method layer. This page gives the design an institutional backbone: how evidence is classified, challenged, corrected, and translated.

Evidence architecture

Claims are classified before they are stylised.

Every publication can use this protocol to separate observed evidence, modelled inference, expert judgement, and editorial interpretation.

01

Observed

Directly supported by a dataset, document, measurement series, interview record, or primary source category.

02

Modelled

Generated by a stated assumption set, transformation, scenario, index, or comparative framework.

03

Inferred

Reasoned from multiple signals where the connection is plausible but not directly measured.

04

Judgement

Named editorial or specialist assessment that should be treated as contingent and reviewable.

Evidence grades

A compact scale for institutional readers.

The grade is not decorative. It tells the reader how much weight to place on a finding in decisions, procurement, planning, or public communication.

GradeBasisUseReview
HighMultiple independent sources, recent data, low sensitivity to assumptions.Suitable for decision memo or public reference.Specialist review
ModerateCredible evidence with gaps, mixed recency, or moderate modelling sensitivity.Useful for planning with explicit caveats.Desk review

Review chain

Publication is a sequence, not a single act.

The review chain helps the site feel credible because it makes intellectual responsibility visible.

  1. 01

    Desk authoring

    Researchers define the question, audience, source classes, and decision relevance.

  2. 02

    Methods check

    Data transformations, assumptions, confidence language, and limitations are challenged before narrative editing.

  3. 03

    Specialist review

    Domain experts review terminology, claim strength, and deployment implications for high-consequence outputs.

  4. 04

    Editorial sign-off

    The final publication is checked for clarity, proportionality, caveats, metadata, and correction readiness.

Uncertainty language

Make uncertainty useful, not evasive.

Uncertainty is most helpful when it changes how a reader should act. These language classes convert caveats into practical interpretation.

A

Measurement uncertainty

Data are incomplete, stale, noisy, geographically uneven, or collected under inconsistent definitions.

B

Model uncertainty

Results depend on assumptions, weights, scenarios, counterfactuals, or translation rules.

C

Behavioural uncertainty

Institutional response, market reaction, public legitimacy, or adoption behaviour is uncertain.

D

Decision uncertainty

Evidence is sufficient to act, but the appropriate threshold, timing, or owner remains contested.

Corrections

Serious publications plan for revision.

A correction policy increases trust because it acknowledges that frontier questions change as new evidence arrives.

01

Errata

Factual, typographic, metadata, or attribution corrections that do not materially change the finding.

02

Material revision

Updates that change interpretation, confidence, scope, or decision relevance.

03

Method note

Post-publication clarifications about assumptions, data treatment, or evidence grading.
