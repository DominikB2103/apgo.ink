Methodology

How Meridian turns evidence into public-facing judgement.

Every publication distinguishes observed fact, modelled inference, expert judgement, and editorial recommendation. Confidence is not decoration; it is part of the claim.

Evidence ladder

Claims are graded before they are amplified.

The ladder is intentionally conservative. It gives readers a visible way to inspect the strength of the underlying record.

  1. A
    High

    Multiple independent sources, recent data, transparent method, low sensitivity to assumptions.

  2. B
    Moderate–high

    Strong triangulation but incomplete coverage, contested definitions, or material uncertainty remains.

  3. C
    Moderate

    Useful directional evidence with explicit caveats and visible assumptions.

  4. D
    Watch only

    Early signal, insufficient source diversity, or high sensitivity to interpretation.

Review process

Four gates before publication.

01

Scope

Define decision relevance, audience, source classes, and explicit exclusions.

02

Build record

Collect sources, document assumptions, classify uncertainty, and produce data notes.

03

Specialist review

Challenge the technical claims, source strength, model choices, and policy interpretation.

04

Editorial sign-off

Separate evidence from judgement and publish corrections when material claims change.

Corrections

Correction posture.

Material updates are logged with date, affected section, reason, and whether the confidence grade changed. Minor typography fixes do not alter the record; evidence changes do.
