Research programmes

Workstreams for connected institutional risk.

Programmes define the institute’s long-horizon agenda. Each desk produces dossiers, memos, data reviews, field notes, and practical translation for institutional decision-makers.

Programme 01

Climate adaptation and infrastructure

The adaptation desk studies how climate pressure interacts with transport, water, power, health, insurance, municipal budgets, and public legitimacy.

Current questions

  • Which infrastructure risks are no longer seasonal?
  • Where do municipal disclosure rules lag physical exposure?
  • How should agencies prioritise adaptation capital?

Primary outputs

  • Exposure dossiers
  • Scenario tables
  • Field notes from municipal agencies

Programme 02

Computational biology and public health

The health desk follows automated discovery pipelines, clinical prediction tools, epidemiological data systems, and the institutional conditions required for responsible deployment.

Current questions

  • What validation evidence is adequate before clinical use?
  • How should subgroup performance be communicated?
  • Which public-health workflows can absorb model uncertainty?

Primary outputs

  • Research letters
  • Benchmark reviews
  • Model assurance checklists

Programme 03

Industrial policy and critical supply

The supply desk maps industrial bottlenecks across semiconductors, battery minerals, food systems, logistics, energy equipment, and strategic manufacturing capacity.

Current questions

  • Which chokepoints are physical, financial, or regulatory?
  • Where do export controls shift rather than solve exposure?
  • How should public incentives measure capacity?

Primary outputs

  • Supply maps
  • Policy memos
  • Procurement risk briefs

Programme 04

AI governance and measurement

The AI desk develops pragmatic evaluation frameworks for high-impact models, public-sector adoption, procurement language, model assurance, and institutional accountability.

Current questions

  • Which benchmark claims survive deployment context?
  • How should contracts preserve audit and escalation rights?
  • What counts as adequate post-deployment monitoring?

Primary outputs

  • Procurement notes
  • Audit frameworks
  • Evaluation memos

Programme 05

Institutional resilience and decision systems

This cross-cutting desk studies how institutions convert evidence into action: escalation thresholds, risk ownership, governance routines, review cadence, and public communication.

Current questions

  • When should a signal change a capital plan?
  • How do executives govern uncertain technical evidence?
  • Which dashboards create false confidence?

Primary outputs

  • Decision protocols
  • Governance reviews
  • Briefing-room materials
