Research programmes

Workstreams for connected institutional risk.

These programme modules are written as a serious public-facing research portfolio: each combines a problem definition, current questions, methods, outputs, and decision relevance.

01 Climate adaptation 02 Computational health 03 Critical supply 04 AI governance 05 Institutional resilience

Programme 01

Climate adaptation and infrastructure

This programme studies the operational systems that determine whether cities, industrial corridors, and public agencies can continue functioning under compounding heat, flood, drought, and energy-system pressure.

Core questions

  • Which infrastructure dependencies fail together?
  • Where are adaptation budgets misaligned with exposure?
  • How should agencies prioritise measurement?

Methods

  • Hazard exposure mapping
  • Institutional interviews
  • Budget and procurement analysis

Programme 02

Computational biology and public health

The health desk tracks automated discovery, clinical prediction, surveillance tools, and evaluation standards where computational systems affect public trust, patient safety, and institutional accountability.

Core questions

  • What minimum evidence should clinical tools meet?
  • How can agencies evaluate model drift?
  • What governance structures preserve accountability?

Outputs

  • Evaluation checklists
  • Deployment risk notes
  • Technical letters for public agencies

Programme 03

Industrial policy and critical supply

This programme maps constraints across semiconductors, energy materials, food systems, medical goods, and other strategically sensitive supply networks.

Core questions

  • Which bottlenecks are physical, financial, or political?
  • What inventories matter for public resilience?
  • How do controls and subsidies change routing behaviour?

Methods

  • Trade-flow analysis
  • Facility-level mapping
  • Scenario workshops

Programme 04

AI governance and measurement

The AI desk focuses on evaluation practices, public-sector procurement, safety assurance, documentation norms, and institutional readiness for high-impact automated systems.

Core questions

  • Which evaluations are decision-relevant?
  • How should public buyers write procurement criteria?
  • What documentation should be mandatory?

Outputs

  • Assurance frameworks
  • Procurement memos
  • Governance briefings

Programme 05

Institutional resilience and decision systems

A cross-cutting programme on how institutions absorb uncertainty: planning cycles, evidence desks, crisis protocols, live dashboards, advisory structures, and procurement governance.

Core questions

  • Where do decision systems lose signal?
  • Which metrics should update continuously?
  • How should leaders review uncertainty?

Methods

  • Decision-flow audits
  • Governance mapping
  • After-action synthesis
