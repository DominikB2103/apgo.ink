Editorial standards

Make the method visible.

Professionalism is not a tone of voice. It is a set of habits readers can inspect.

PolicyLabelsSourcesCorrectionsImageryFunding

APGO’s standards apply to journalism, essays, rankings and public tools. The format may change; the obligation to show the basis of a claim does not.

Label the kind of work

Reporting, analysis, opinion, essay, evidence note and methodology are not interchangeable. Each piece should tell readers what they are entering and distinguish verified facts from interpretation.

Put evidence beside argument

Whenever practical, APGO links directly to primary documents, official datasets, research papers and original texts. Secondary reporting can supply context but should not become an invisible chain of recycled claims. Uncertainty and important limitations stay in the article.

Correct in public

Material factual errors are corrected in the article and recorded on the corrections page with the date and substance of the change. Minor typography fixes need not be logged. Methodology revisions remain versioned and explained.

Documentary and generated imagery

Illustration is not evidence. APGO may use art-directed imagery created or substantially assisted with generative tools. It must be presented as editorial illustration, never as a documentary photograph, and may not fabricate a real event, person or source. Documentary images, when used, should carry provenance and an accurate caption.

Funding and conflicts

Reader funding does not purchase editorial influence. Relevant financial, personal or institutional conflicts should be disclosed with the work. Rankings will publish their weights, data windows, known gaps and model changes.

Current limits

APGO is an early-stage independent project. It does not yet operate a secure tip channel, membership account system or full-time newsroom. The site will not claim those services until they exist and have been tested.
