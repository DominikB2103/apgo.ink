World Player Index · Method v0.2

No mystery ballot. No reputation points.

The APGO World Player Index will use a rolling 365-day window, role-specific baselines and a visible confidence band. It is not live yet: a pilot top 25 will ship only after licensed data, multi-season backtesting and outside review are in place.

About “official”

No governing body has authorised APGO. The goal is to build the most transparent independent reference—not to borrow authority the project has not earned.

Method v0.2

From raw event to public score

Designed for scrutiny, not false precision
01

Define coverage

Publish the competitions, providers, missing leagues and minimum 900-minute eligibility rule before naming a player.

02

Compare roles

Evaluate goalkeepers, centre-backs, full-backs, midfield roles, wide attackers and strikers against their own jobs first.

03

Adjust context

Account for possession, game state, opponent strength, minutes and opportunity instead of simply adding per-90 counts.

04

Weight recency

Use a 120-day half-life, capped match-importance multipliers and a transparent team-strength model.

05

Shrink uncertainty

Pull small samples toward the midpoint and show a confidence band, provisional badge and coverage score.

06

Archive every release

Publish weights, model version, changes, corrections and downloadable outputs wherever the data licence permits.

Proposed aggregationIndex = 50 + 10 × confidence × weighted role score

Confidence rises with rolling competitive minutes and stops a brilliant cameo from outranking a year of sustained performance.

Launch path

Build credibility before scale

  1. Data & definitionsSecure lawful event coverage, publish the dictionary and test league/role bias.
  2. Backtest & reviewRun several completed seasons and invite independent football analysts to challenge the results.
  3. Pilot top 25Release scores, uncertainty, minutes, role tables and plain-language placement notes.
  4. Quarterly indexAdd player histories and comparisons only after the pipeline is reproducible.
Build the standard

Fund open football research.

Support goes to lawful data access, engineering and independent review—not a preferred ranking.
